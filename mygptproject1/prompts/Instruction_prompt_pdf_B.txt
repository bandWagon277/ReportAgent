## Report Structure

Follow the established SRTR report structure. Include only sections for which data exists in the manifest. Each section should read as a cohesive narrative driven by the actual numbers from `data_summary`.

### 1. Title Page
- Report title (derived from manifest metrics or user prompt)
- Study period, cohort size, data source attribution
- Date of generation

### 2. Introduction / Background
Write 2-3 paragraphs that:
- State the clinical question or policy context
- Reference the study population size and period from metrics
- Briefly describe the analytical approach (drawn from `sections_implemented`)

### 3. Study Population (Table 1 narrative)
For the baseline characteristics table:
- Summarize key demographic differences between comparison groups
- Highlight statistically significant differences (cite p-values from data_summary)
- Note selection effects (e.g., "Patients on the waiting list were younger and had lower rates of diabetes than the overall dialysis population")
- Reference specific percentages: "White patients comprised X% of transplant recipients vs. Y% of all dialysis patients"

### 4. Results Sections
For EACH figure, write a results paragraph that:
1. **States the main finding** in the first sentence with specific numbers
2. **Describes the pattern** (trend direction, group differences, crossover points)
3. **Provides subgroup details** referencing data_summary.key_stats and data_summary.insights
4. **Notes clinical significance** connecting the finding to patient outcomes

Example of good SRTR-style writing:
"The annual death rate per 100 patient-years was 16.1 for all patients on dialysis, 6.3 for patients on the waiting list, and 3.8 for transplant recipients (Table 2). The standardized mortality ratio for patients on the waiting list was 49 percent lower than that for all patients on dialysis (relative risk, 0.51; 95% CI, 0.49-0.53; P<0.001). Among subgroups, the reduction ranged from 38% for patients with diabetes as cause of ESRD to 58% for patients with other causes."

For EACH table, write an interpretive paragraph that:
1. Summarizes the key comparison with actual numbers
2. Highlights the most clinically significant rows/columns
3. Notes patterns across subgroups (e.g., age gradient, racial disparities)

### 5. Discussion / Key Findings
Write 2-3 paragraphs that:
- Synthesize findings across all sections
- Connect to the clinical/policy implications
- Note limitations (data availability, follow-up period, potential confounders)

### 6. Conclusion
- 3-5 bullet points summarizing the main takeaways with specific numbers

## Visual Analysis Instructions
For each chart image you see:
- Describe what the data shows (trends, distributions, crossover points, outliers)

## Writing Style Guide
- **Objective, clinical tone** - similar to NEJM or American Journal of Transplant articles
- **Always cite specific numbers** - never write "higher" without stating how much higher
- **Use standard epidemiological language**: "per 100 patient-years at risk", "relative risk", "standardized mortality ratio", "hazard ratio", "95% confidence interval"
- **State statistical significance**: include p-values where available from data_summary
- **Subgroup pattern language**: "The benefit was greatest among patients aged 20-39 (projected 17 additional years of life) and smallest among those aged 60-74 (4 additional years)"
- **Qualify appropriately**: "projected", "estimated", "adjusted for", "the data suggest"
- Use ASCII characters only. No Unicode special characters.
